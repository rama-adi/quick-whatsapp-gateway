---
title: Organizations
description: How resources are owned, and how to share a connection with others.
---

Every WhatsApp number, API key, and webhook belongs to an **organization** — not
to a person directly. People reach a resource through their **membership** in the
org that owns it. This is what lets several people co-manage the same WhatsApp
connection.

## Your personal organization

When you sign up, you get a personal organization automatically, and you are its
owner. For solo use it is invisible: everything you create goes into it, and you
never have to think about orgs at all.

It becomes useful the moment you want a second person on a connection.

## Sharing a connection

To let a teammate manage one of your numbers, you don't hand over your login or
copy an API key — you **invite them into the organization** that owns the number.
Once they accept, they are a member and see that org's sessions, keys, and events.

Members have a role that sets what they can do:

| Role | Can do |
|---|---|
| `owner` | Everything, including managing members and deleting the org. |
| `admin` | Manage the org's sessions, keys, and webhooks, and invite people. |
| `member` | Read and send, but not change settings or membership. |

A person can belong to more than one organization. The dashboard has an **org
switcher** to choose which one you're acting in; whatever org is active is the one
your tokens and new resources belong to.

<Callout type="info">
**Inviting from the UI is a fast-follow.** The underlying support for members,
roles, and email invitations is in place, but the dashboard screens for sending
and accepting invitations land in a follow-up release. Until then, organizations
work for solo use out of the box, and the invitation flow becomes available
through the dashboard once that ships.
</Callout>

## Platform admin vs org roles

Two different kinds of "admin" exist, and they don't overlap:

- **Org roles** (`owner`, `admin`, `member`) decide what you can do *inside one
  organization*.
- The **platform super admin** — the first account created — sits above all orgs:
  it manages users and can see every organization's sessions for oversight. See
  [the dashboard](/docs/guides/dashboard).
